Clownfish

The clownfish, also known as anemonefish, is a small tropical fish known for its vibrant orange color and unique symbiotic relationship with sea anemones. These fascinating creatures are immune to the stinging tentacles of anemones, using them as protection from predators. Clownfish live in small groups led by a dominant female, and they exhibit sequential hermaphroditism - if the female dies, the dominant male will change sex to become the new female.

Native to the warm waters of the Indian and Pacific Oceans, clownfish have gained worldwide popularity after the release of the animated film "Finding Nemo". They typically grow to about 4.3 inches in length and feed on small invertebrates and algae. Their bright coloration serves as warning to potential predators about their toxic host anemones.
